Velo Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 7, 2026

In short Velo is a home screen launcher. We do not collect, sell, or transmit your personal data to our servers. The app does not use the network, does not include analytics or advertising, and keeps your information on your device.

This Privacy Policy describes how Flutteroid (“we”, “us”, or “our”) handles information when you use Velo (the “App”), an Android home screen launcher published under the package name com.flutteroid.velo.

By using Velo, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Information we process

Velo processes information only as needed to provide launcher features. All processing happens locally on your device unless you explicitly choose an action that opens another app (for example, a web search in your browser).

Installed applications

To show your app drawer, search apps, and launch applications, Velo reads information about apps installed on your device, such as app names, package names, and icons. This information is used only within the App and is not sent to us or third parties.

Launcher preferences and settings

Velo stores your customization choices on your device, including:

  • Theme, font, background, and appearance settings
  • Favorite and hidden apps
  • Home screen widget selections
  • Onboarding completion status
  • Recent search queries, if you leave that feature enabled

This data is saved using Android local storage (DataStore preferences) and remains on your device.

Contacts (optional)

If you grant the Contacts permission, Velo can search your contacts locally to help you find and open them from search. Contact data is read from your device, processed on-device, and is never uploaded to us or shared with third parties by Velo.

You can deny or revoke this permission at any time in your device settings. Search will continue to work for apps and other features without contact access.

Notifications (optional)

If you enable notification access for Velo in Android system settings, the App can read active notifications to display badges and notification previews on the home screen. Notification content is kept in memory while the service is connected and is cleared when notification access is disabled or the service disconnects. Velo does not store notification content in persistent storage or transmit it off your device.

Widgets

When you add third-party widgets to your home screen, those widgets run within Velo but are provided by their respective app developers. Data handled by a widget is governed by that widget provider’s privacy practices, not this policy.

2. What we do not do

  • No account required. Velo does not ask you to sign in.
  • No analytics or tracking. We do not use analytics SDKs, advertising identifiers, or behavioral tracking.
  • No network access. Velo is designed to operate without internet access. The App does not connect to Flutteroid servers.
  • No sale of personal data. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information.

3. Actions that leave Velo

Some features intentionally open other apps on your device:

  • Web search opens your default browser or search app. Any data sent over the internet is handled by that app and its provider, not by Velo.
  • Launching apps, contacts, or system settings opens the selected destination under that app’s or the operating system’s privacy rules.

4. Permissions

Velo may request or rely on the following permissions:

  • Query all packages — required on recent Android versions so Velo can discover and display installed applications.
  • Bind app widgets — allows Velo to host home screen widgets you choose.
  • Read contacts — optional; used only for on-device contact search if you grant it.
  • Notification listener access — optional; granted by you in system settings for notification badges and previews.

Velo does not request location, camera, microphone, SMS, call log, or storage access for its core launcher features.

5. Data retention and deletion

Locally stored preferences remain on your device until you change or remove them, disable features such as recent searches, or uninstall Velo. Uninstalling the App removes Velo’s local app data from your device, subject to Android backup behavior described below.

6. Android backup and device transfer

Velo supports Android’s backup framework. Depending on your device and Google account settings, some app preferences may be included in encrypted device backups or device-to-device transfers managed by Google or your device manufacturer. We do not receive access to those backups.

7. Children’s privacy

Velo is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through Velo in a way that concerns you, please contact us using the details below.

8. Security

Because Velo keeps data on your device and does not operate backend servers for the App, your information is protected primarily by your device’s operating system and security settings. We recommend keeping your device updated and using a screen lock.

9. International users

Velo is built to process data locally on your device. If you use features that open external apps or services, those services may process data according to their own policies and locations.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of Velo after changes become effective means you accept the updated policy.
